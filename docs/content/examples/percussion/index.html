--- title: "Percussion Notation" description: "Adding mridangam and tabla patterns to your notation" ---

While the Notations library primarily focuses on melodic content, you can annotate percussion patterns alongside your swaras. This guide shows various approaches.

Basic Approach: Using Comments

The simplest way is to add percussion as comments or labels:

\cycle("|4|2|2|") \beatDuration(4) \line("Pallavi with Mridangam") Sw: S R G M , P D N S. , ___ ___ , ___ ___ | \line("Mridangam: ta ka dhi mi , ta ka jha nu , ___ ___ , ___ ___")

Separate Percussion Line

Create a dedicated line for percussion patterns:

\cycle("|4|2|2|") \beatDuration(4) \line("Melody") Sw: S R G M , P D N S. , R. S. N D , P M G R | \line("Mridangam") Pe: ta ka dhi mi , ta ka jha nu , ta ki ta ta , ka dhi mi ta |

Mridangam Korvai Patterns

Korvai are rhythmic patterns, typically in groups of 3:

\cycle("|4|2|2|") \beatDuration(4) \line("Korvai - First Iteration") Pe: ta ka dhi mi ta ka jha nu , ta ri ki ta ta ka dhi mi , ta ka jha nu ___ , ___ ___ ___ ___ | \line("Korvai - Second Iteration") Pe: ta ka dhi mi ta ka jha nu , ta ri ki ta ta ka dhi mi , ta ka jha nu ___ , ___ ___ ___ ___ | \line("Korvai - Third Iteration") Pe: ta ka dhi mi ta ka jha nu , ta ri ki ta ta ka dhi mi , ta ka jha nu ___ , ___ ___ ___ ___ | \line("Mohra (Ending)") Pe: ta ___ dhi mi ___ , ___ ta ___ , ___ ___ ___ ||

Tabla Patterns

For Hindustani music contexts:

\cycle("|4|4|4|4|") \beatDuration(4) \line("Melody") Sw: S R G M , P D N S. , R. G. M. P. , D. N. S.. R.. | \line("Tabla (Teental)") Pe: dha dhin dhin dha , dha dhin dhin dha , dha tin tin ta , ta dhin dhin dha |

Notation Symbols

Common mridangam syllables (solkattu):

  • ta, ki, ta - Right hand (treble) strokes
  • tha, thom, nam - Left hand (bass) strokes
  • dhi, mi - Combined strokes
  • ka, ra - Finger rolls
  • jha, nu - Combined patterns

Practical Tips

  • Use a separate line prefixed with Pe: for percussion
  • Align percussion syllables with corresponding melody beats
  • Mark korvai iterations clearly (1st, 2nd, 3rd)
  • Use ___ for rests or sustained tones
  • Study traditional korvai patterns and their variations
  • Practice reciting solkattu (vocal percussion syllables)
  • Consider the interaction between melody and percussion
