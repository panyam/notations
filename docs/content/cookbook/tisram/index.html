--- title: "Tisram Notation" description: "Working with 3 notes per beat patterns (tisram gati)" ---

Tisram (3 notes per beat) is one of the five gatis (rhythmic patterns) in Carnatic music. This guide shows how to notate tisram patterns effectively.

Understanding Tisram

In Adi Tala (8 beats), tisram means fitting 3 notes into each beat, creating a triplet feel:

\cycle("|4|2|2|") \beatDuration(4) \line("Tisram - 3 notes per beat") SRG MPD NS. R.G. , M.P. D.N. S..R.. G..M.. , P..D.. N..S... ___ ___ , ___ ___ ___ ___

Tisram vs Chatusram (4 notes per beat)

Comparison of the same phrase in different gatis:

\cycle("|4|2|2|") \beatDuration(4) \line("Chatusram (4 per beat)") SRGM PDNS. R.G.M. P.D.N. , S..R..G..M.. P..D..N..S... , ___ ___ , ___ ___ \line("Tisram (3 per beat)") SRG MPD NS. R.G. , M.P. D.N. S..R.. G..M.. , P..D.. N..S... ___ , ___ ___ ___

Kanda Chapu (5 beats) - Natural Tisram Feel

Kanda Chapu naturally accommodates tisram patterns:

\cycle("|2|3|") \beatDuration(4) SRG MPD , NS. R.G. M.P. | D.N. S..R.. , G..M.. P..D.. N..S...

Creating Tisram Phrases

Ascending Patterns

\cycle("|4|2|2|") \beatDuration(4) SRG RGM GMP MPD , PDN DNS. NS.R. S.R.G. , R.G.M. G.M.P. M.P.D. P.D.N. , D.N.S.. ___ ___ ___

Descending Patterns

\cycle("|4|2|2|") \beatDuration(4) S..N..D.. N..D..P.. D..P..M.. P..M..G.. , M..G..R.. G..R..S.. R..S..N. S..N.D , ND.P. D.PM PM.G , MGR. RS.N NPD.

Mixed Patterns

\cycle("|4|2|2|") \beatDuration(4) SRG SRG MGP MGP , PDN PDN S.R.G. S.R.G. , M.P.D. M.P.D. N.S..R.. N.S..R.. , G..M..P.. ___ ___ ___

Tisram with Gamakas

Adding embellishments to tisram patterns:

\cycle("|4|2|2|") \beatDuration(4) (SRG kampitam) MPD NS.R.G. (M.P.D. jaru N.S..) , (S..D..N.. kampitam) N..S..P.. P..M..G.. (MGR jaru S) , ___ ___ ___ ___ , ___ ___ ___ ___

Converting from Chatusram to Tisram

Take a phrase in chatusram and convert it to tisram:

\cycle("|4|2|2|") \beatDuration(4) \line("Original (Chatusram - 4 per beat)") SRGM PDNS. R.G.M.P. D.N.S..R.. , ___ ___ ___ ___ , ___ ___ ___ ___ , ___ ___ ___ ___ \line("Converted (Tisram - 3 per beat)") SRG MPD NS. R.G. , M.P. D.N. S..R.. G..M.. , P..D.. N..S... ___ ___ , ___ ___ ___ ___

Practical Tips

  • Tisram creates a triplet feel - think "1-and-a, 2-and-a, 3-and-a"
  • Practice with a metronome to internalize the 3-against-4 feel
  • Kanda Chapu (5 beats) is especially suited for tisram
  • Use tisram strategically to create rhythmic interest
  • Combine with other gatis (chatusram, khanda) for variety
  • Mark tisram sections clearly when notating
